What's Happening?

The Village of Lemont Planning & Zoning Commission reviews proposed changes to how land can be used in our community. A rezoning proposal has been submitted that affects properties near residential neighborhoods in our area, including the Estates of Montefiori and surrounding single-family home communities.

Rezoning changes what types of buildings and activities are allowed on specific parcels of land. These decisions can have lasting impacts on neighboring areas, affecting traffic patterns, property values, and the character of our established residential communities.

What is Rezoning?

Rezoning is the process of changing the designated use of a property from one zoning classification to another. For example, land currently zoned for single-family homes might be rezoned for multi-family housing, commercial use, or other purposes. This change affects what can be built, how tall buildings can be, parking requirements, and more.

Your Voice Matters

Public input during Planning & Zoning Commission meetings is a critical part of the decision-making process. Commissioners consider resident concerns when making recommendations to the Village Board.

How the Process Works

  • 1
    Application Submitted: A property owner or developer submits a rezoning request to the Village's Community Development Department.
  • 2
    Staff Review: Village staff analyzes the proposal, checking compliance with the Comprehensive Plan and evaluating potential impacts.
  • 3
    Public Hearing: The Planning & Zoning Commission holds a public hearing where residents can voice concerns, ask questions, and provide input.
  • 4
    Commission Recommendation: After considering public input and staff analysis, the Commission makes a recommendation to approve, deny, or modify the rezoning request.
  • 5
    Village Board Decision: The Village Board of Trustees makes the final decision on the rezoning proposal.

What You Can Do

Community participation in local government is one of the most powerful tools residents have to protect their investments and maintain quality of life in our neighborhoods. Your voice matters in shaping the future of our community.

Attend the Meeting

Show up to the Planning & Zoning Commission meeting. Your physical presence demonstrates community concern and engagement. Meetings are held at Village Hall, 418 Main Street, Lemont.

Speak During Public Comment

Public hearings include time for resident comments. Prepare a brief statement about your concerns. Be specific about how the rezoning might affect you, your neighbors, and the community.

Review Meeting Materials

Agendas and supporting documents are posted at least 48 hours before meetings on the Village website. Review the rezoning application details before attending.

Contact the Village

Reach out to the Community Development Department at (630) 257-1595 with questions. You can also contact Village Trustees to express your views.

Organize with Neighbors

Connect with fellow residents from surrounding neighborhoods. A unified voice from the community carries more weight than individual comments. Consider forming a coalition of concerned neighbors.

Submit Written Comments

If you cannot attend in person, submit written comments to the Community Development Department. Written testimony becomes part of the official record.

Stay Informed & Make Your Voice Heard

Access official meeting information, agendas, and contact the Village to learn more about how this rezoning proposal could affect our community and surrounding residential neighborhoods.
